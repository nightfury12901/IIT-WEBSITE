Turbulent Resilient Free Space Optical System

Designed and developed at IIT Delhi for high-reliability, high-throughput wireless optical communication

Project Overview

Free-Space Optical (FSO) communication has emerged as a compelling solution for high-speed, secure, and license-free wireless data transmission. By utilizing laser beams as the transmission medium, FSO enables higher data throughput, reduced beam divergence, and more compact and lightweight transceiver hardware, all while consuming less power.

Exploiting these features, our team at IIT Delhi has indigenously developed a novel turbulence resilient FSO transceiver prototype featuring advanced beam engineering and a unified, fully automatic system for pointing, acquisition, and tracking (PAT), along with real-time network monitoring. Designed for operation in the C-band, our prototype targets data rates up to 10 Gbps over distances up to 20 km, achieving 99% link reliability with a link establishment time of under 30 minutes. These features make the system ideal for deployment in remote and challenging environments such as mountainous regions, rural areas, campus connectivity setups, and wireless camera links to centralized control rooms.

What is different about our system?

  • Commercial FSO systems use conventional laser beams, requiring manual/semi-automated alignment (bulky and slow).
  • Atmospheric turbulence leads to scintillation, beam spreading and beam wandering, degrading performance for long-range links.
  • We engineered the laser beam to mitigate scintillation, enhancing link availability and performance in difficult conditions.
  • Automated coarse- and fine-alignment mechanisms (eliminating continuous manual intervention), ensuring robust, stable links.
  • System is fully compatible with secure communications (fiber, wireless, mixed infrastructure).
